1NIT AgartalaTripura
      Nit Agartala       
         -
Ujjayanta Palace:
Located about 15-20 km (a 30-minute drive) from the NIT campus in Agartala,
        this former royal palace features grand architecture, beautiful Mughal-style gardens,
        and houses the Tripura Government Museum.
        -
Heritage Park:
Situated roughly 14 km away in Kunjaban, this park offers a miniature view of
        Tripura’s diverse cultural and natural landscapes. It is a peaceful spot frequently visited for leisurely study breaks.
        -
Sepahijala Wildlife Sanctuary:
Located about 25 km south of NIT Agartala (approx. 45 minutes by road),
        this reserve features a botanical garden, a zoo, a boating lake,
        and is famous for being home to the spectacled monkey and clouded leopard.
        -
Akhaura Integrated Checkpost:
About 10 km from campus, this is the India-Bangladesh border crossing.
        Students frequently visit to watch the daily evening "beating retreat" flag-lowering ceremony.
        -
Neermahal (Water Palace):
Situated about 55 km from NIT (roughly a 1.5-hour drive) on
        Rudrasagar Lake in Melaghar, this stunning former royal summer
        resort is the only water palace in Eastern India.
        -
Tripura Sundari Temple (Matabari):
Located in Udaipur, about 55 km from NIT (approx. 1.5 hours),
        this is one of the 51 revered Shakti Peethas in India.
